Understanding Common Bank and Credit Union Fees

Fees are an important factor when choosing a financial institution. This overview lists general types of fees you may encounter. Actual dollar amounts vary significantly between providers like Citibank, Navy Federal, and SchoolsFirst and depend on the specific account type you hold.

Key Fee Categories

Fee Type Description Average Range (Approximate)
Monthly Maintenance Fee A regular charge to keep a checking or savings account open. Often waived if certain conditions (e.g., minimum balance, direct deposit) are met. \$5 - \$25 per month
Overdraft Fee (ODF) Incurred when a transaction exceeds the available balance in your checking account. Many institutions offer overdraft protection options. \$25 - \$36 per transaction
Non-Network ATM Fee A fee charged by your bank for using an Automated Teller Machine (ATM) outside of their partner network. The owner of the ATM may also charge a separate fee. \$2.50 - \$5.00 per transaction
Wire Transfer Fee A charge for electronically sending funds to another bank account, particularly internationally. \$15 - \$50 per transfer
Stop Payment Fee A fee for requesting the bank to cancel a specific check or pre-authorized payment before it clears. \$20 - \$35 per request

Important Compliance Warning

This site does not publish the current, official fee schedules for any specific bank or credit union. The ranges provided above are for general informational purposes only and are subject to change without notice. Always refer to the official "Disclosure of Fees" or "Schedule of Charges" documents on the official websites of institutions like America First Credit Union or Bank of America before opening any account.
